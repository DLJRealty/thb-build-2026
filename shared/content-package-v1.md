## 6. AREA GUIDE (`/woodland-park-area-guide/`)

**H1:** Woodland Park: The City Above the Clouds

**Body (~410 words):**

Woodland Park calls itself "The City Above the Clouds," and it isn't marketing bravado — at 8,500 feet, you'll occasionally look *down* at the weather. Perched on the northwest shoulder of Pikes Peak, this small mountain town delivers the rarest combination in Colorado travel: genuine high-country escape, just 25 minutes from Colorado Springs and Manitou Springs.

**Pikes Peak — America's Mountain.**
The 14,115-foot giant that inspired "America the Beautiful" dominates every horizon here — including the view from several of our homes. Drive the Pikes Peak Highway to the summit, ride the historic cog railway from Manitou Springs, or hike sections of Barr Trail if your legs are feeling ambitious. Pro tip: summit weather changes fast, so mornings are your friend.

**Garden of the Gods.**
Twenty-five minutes down Ute Pass, 300-foot sandstone fins glow red against blue Colorado sky. It's free, it's stunning, and the paved central paths make it easy for every fitness level. Go at golden hour and thank us later.

**Summer (June–September).**
This is trail season. Hundreds of miles of hiking and biking thread through Pike National Forest, from mellow strolls around Manitou Lake to lung-busting summit routes. Afternoons often bring brief mountain thunderstorms — start early, then reward yourself in Woodland Park's cafés and breweries.

**Fall (late September–October).**
The aspens turn, and the hillsides around Woodland Park go molten gold. It's brief — usually two to three weeks — and worth planning a trip around. The drives along Highway 67 toward Cripple Creek are pure gold-season theater.

**Winter (November–March).**
Snow settles in, crowds thin out, and the town turns quiet and bright. Think snowshoeing through silent pines, sledding hills, mule deer in the meadows, and long evenings by the gas fire pit with cocoa in hand. The roads are well-maintained, and Colorado Springs stays an easy 25 minutes away.

**After dark — every season.**
At this altitude, with this little light pollution, the night sky is a main event. The Milky Way on a clear moonless night here stops first-time visitors mid-sentence. Step outside, let your eyes adjust for ten minutes, and look up.

**In town.**
Woodland Park itself has everything a getaway needs: local coffee, family-run restaurants, a Saturday farmers market in summer, and the Rocky Mountain Dinosaur Resource Center for the kids (and, honestly, the adults).

Base yourself here, and all of it — summit to sandstone to starlight — is within half an hour.

## 7. FAQ (`/faq/`)

**H1:** Good Questions, Straight Answers

**1. How does check-in work?**
Check-in is easy and self-guided — no front desk, no waiting on a key handoff. Before your arrival, we'll send simple, step-by-step arrival instructions so you can settle in smoothly, whenever your travel day gets you here. If anything's unclear, message us and you'll hear back fast.

**2. Are the fire pits wood-burning?**
No — and that's by design. Every fire pit at our homes is **gas**, which means instant flame, no smoke in your clothes, no embers to babysit, and no hauling anything. Turn it on, gather around, turn it off. Please never bring or burn wood at any of our homes — gas only, always.

**3. Can we use the fire pit during fire restrictions?**
Gas fire pits are typically permitted when wood fires are restricted, which is one of the reasons ours are gas-only. That said, restrictions can change with conditions — during your stay we'll always let you know if anything is temporarily off-limits, and current local guidance always applies.

**4. Which homes have grills?**
Four of our stays have propane grill access: **Boutique TinyHome w/Direct View of PikesPeak!**, **Pikes Peak Boutique Retreat**, and **Pikes Peak BrightStar Boutique** share a propane grill located steps from those three homes, and our upcoming **Woodland Park Home with Direct Views of Pikes Peak!** has its own propane grill. Because they run on propane rather than charcoal or wood, they're typically usable even during fire restrictions.

**5. Are pets allowed?**
Pet policies vary by home. Please check the house rules on your specific listing before booking, or just message us — we'll give you a straight answer for the home you're eyeing.

**6. What's your cancellation policy?**
Policies are listed on each home's booking page so there are no surprises. If your plans are uncertain, reach out before you book — we're happy to walk you through how it works for your dates.

**7. How do we get there, and is parking included?**
All of our homes are in Woodland Park, Colorado — about 25 minutes from Colorado Springs and Manitou Springs via Highway 24 up Ute Pass, a beautiful drive in its own right. You'll receive exact directions before arrival, and every home has free on-site parking. In winter, the highway is well-maintained; normal snow-season driving awareness is all you need.

**8. Should we worry about the altitude?**
Woodland Park sits at about 8,500 feet, and most guests feel nothing more than great views and better stars. A few easy tips for your first day: drink more water than usual, go a little lighter on alcohol and caffeine, and save the big hike for day two. If you're sensitive to altitude, an extra-easy first evening by the fire pit is the perfect prescription.

**9. Is a tiny home comfortable for a real vacation?**
Genuinely, yes — it's our whole craft. Each home is designed so every inch works: real beds, real kitchens and bathrooms, quality linens, and interiors our guests routinely describe as nicer than the photos. And if you want more room, our **Woodland Peak Tiny Lodge** sleeps up to six, and a full home with direct Pikes Peak views is opening soon.

**10. Why should I book direct instead of through a booking platform?**
Three reasons: you deal directly with your hosts (fast, personal answers instead of a platform queue), you skip the third-party service fees platforms add on top, and direct guests get first access to new homes and newly opened dates. Same homes, same Superhost care, better experience.

**11. Is booking direct on this site secure and trustworthy?**
Yes. You're booking with the same family-run team behind an Airbnb Superhost profile with 1,000+ five-star reviews — just without the middleman. Your booking is confirmed directly with us, and we're personally reachable before, during, and after your stay.

**12. What if we need something during our stay?**
Message us — any hour. Fast, personal response is the thing our reviews mention most, and it's the standard we hold around the clock. Whether it's a question about the fire pit, a restaurant recommendation, or anything unexpected, a real answer comes back in minutes, not days.
